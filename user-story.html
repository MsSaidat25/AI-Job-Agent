The User Journey

First visit to "9 jobs applied, 1 needs your tap." Click any step to expand.

0 Signup & Onboarding

Three ways in, nobody left behind:

  • "I have a resume" → Upload it (Step 1)
  • "I don't have a resume" → The agent walks you through questions about your education, projects, and skills. Builds your first resume from the conversation.
  • "I just want to look around" → Jump straight to the job feed with a bare-bones profile. You'll need to fill it in before you can apply.
You connect your email (Gmail or Outlook, via OAuth) during signup. This is what powers autonomous apply and response tracking. You can use your main address or set up a +jobs alias to keep things separate.
Platform — Mobile-first web app, built as a PWA. Works on your phone, tablet, or laptop. No app store, no install, no CLI.
1 Upload Resume & Smart Parse

Hand it a PDF, a Word doc, or even a photo of a printed resume. The AI pulls out everything:

  • Contact info, location, languages
  • Work history with dates, titles, and what you actually did
  • Skills, mapped to O*NET and ESCO taxonomies
  • Education and certifications
Everything's pre-filled but you can edit any of it. Fields the parser isn't sure about get flagged. Your profile sticks around across sessions and devices.
Parsing — Claude Vision for the hard stuff (scanned pages, multi-column layouts, non-English resumes). Gemini Flash for clean text extraction.
2 Know Your Worth (Market Calibration)

Before you start applying anywhere, the agent compares your resume against real market data and tells you what you should be targeting:

  • Market arbitrage — "Your skills pull $95K remote from Lagos, $140K in Berlin, $180K in San Francisco"
  • Level calibration — "You qualify for Senior. You're searching Mid-level. Aim higher."
  • Salary range — Your floor, your target, and your stretch number, broken out by region
  • Country opportunities — "The Netherlands has strong demand for your stack, good visa sponsorship, and pays about 20% more"
Data — Levels.fyi API for tech roles, salary bands pulled from live job postings via aggregator APIs, plus Claude analysis layered on top. Every number shows its source and when it was last updated. Refreshed monthly.
3 Skill Gap Intelligence

The agent looks at thousands of live job postings for your target roles and tells you exactly where you stand:

  • Must-have gaps — "85% of Senior Data Engineer roles ask for Spark. You don't have it listed."
  • Nice-to-have gaps — "Kubernetes shows up in 40% of postings but almost never as a hard requirement. Don't stress about it."
  • Hidden strengths — "Only 12% of candidates have Airflow experience. You do. Lead with that."
  • Upskill ROI — "Getting an AWS cert would bump your match rate by 30% and move you up about $15K. Takes about 6 weeks."
  • Learning paths — Specific courses, certs, and time estimates
Every recommendation comes with a dollar figure. "Learning X is worth $Y more per year." No vague advice.
4 Job Discovery (All Sources, One Feed)

Jobs from a bunch of different sources, all in one place, deduplicated:

  • Aggregator APIs — Adzuna, JSearch, Jooble, Reed, Arbeitnow, Remotive, The Muse
  • Paste a URL — Found something on LinkedIn? Paste the link. The agent grabs the listing, scores it, and adds it to your feed.
  • Company watchlist — Pick companies you like and the agent monitors their careers pages for new openings
  • If the same job appears on 3 different boards, you see it once with all three sources listed
  • Every listing gets a 0-100 score with a plain-language explanation of why
  • New matches show up daily via email or push notification
"You're only looking at Data Analyst roles, but your profile is a really good fit for Analytics Engineer too. Want me to pull those in?"
5 Per-Job Tailored Resumes & Cover Letters

Every application gets its own resume. Not one generic version sent to 50 companies:

  • Keywords pulled straight from the job description so it passes ATS filters
  • Your achievements reordered and reframed based on what this specific role cares about
  • Cover letter with a real hook about the company — their recent funding round, their tech stack, whatever's relevant
  • Export as PDF, DOCX, or Markdown
  • Tailoring notes show you exactly what changed and why ("Pushed Python and SQL to the top, pulled Java down — this is a data pipeline role")
  • Nothing gets made up. The agent only uses what's in your verified profile.
Over time, the agent figures out which versions work best. "Your technical-tone resumes get twice as many callbacks for backend roles."
Model — Claude Sonnet handles all the writing through OpenRouter. This is the part where quality really matters. Everything else runs on Gemini to keep costs low.
6 Autonomous Apply (Works While You Sleep) V3 CORE

Every matched job gets a confidence score, and the agent knows which submission channels are safe to use on its own vs. which ones need you to tap approve.

Goes out automatically on safe channels. Gets queued on risky ones for your one-tap approval.

Everything's prepared but the agent holds off and asks you first. Docs are ready to go if you say yes.

SKIP Below 50%. Not worth your time. Still visible in the "Skipped" tab if you want to see why.
FULLY AUTONOMOUS

Email applications — Lots of jobs say "send your resume to careers@company.com." The agent writes the email, attaches your tailored resume and cover letter, and sends it from your connected email. No platform involved, totally legal.

Company career pages — Submits directly through the company's own application form. No job board ToS to worry about.

Open API boards — Arbeitnow, Remotive, and others that have proper APIs for accepting applications.

QUEUED — One-Tap Approval

LinkedIn, Indeed, Workday, iCIMS, Greenhouse — The agent builds a tailored resume, fills out every form field, and saves it all as a draft. In the morning you open the app, see everything ready, and batch-approve in a few taps.

Why not just auto-submit these too? Because these platforms actively detect bots. CAPTCHAs, 2FA, ML detection. Automating them gets accounts banned. Your one tap per application keeps you safe.

⚡ Your Morning Briefing Today, 7:02 AM
6 email applications sent — tailored resume + cover letter for each
3 career page applications sent — forms filled and submitted
2 LinkedIn Easy Apply queued — pre-filled, just needs your tap
1 new response — Stripe wants to set up an interview
You pick the confidence threshold. Set it to 90% if you want tight control, drop it to 70% if you want volume. The agent watches what you approve and reject over time and adjusts. You can also just turn autonomous mode off whenever you want.
7 Response Tracking & Pipeline
  • Every application gets tracked through the pipeline: submitted → under review → interview → offer or rejected
  • Email monitoring via OAuth — The agent reads incoming emails from employers and figures out what they are (rejection, interview invite, follow-up request, offer). Your pipeline updates on its own.
  • Interview spotted? "You've got an interview at Stripe, Thursday 2pm. Want me to add it to your calendar? Here's a prep doc for this role."
  • Ghosted for 7 days? The agent drafts a follow-up email for you to look over before sending.
  • All employer feedback gets logged and analyzed across your full application history
How email works — Gmail API with gmail.readonly and gmail.labels scopes. The agent sets up a "Job Applications" label and only watches that label. It never touches your personal inbox. You can revoke access whenever.
8 AI Career Advisor

This isn't just something that fills out forms. It's a strategic advisor that gets better the more you use it:

  • Weekly brief — "You applied to 34 jobs this week. Got 8 callbacks, that's 24%, up from 15% last week. Fintech is clearly your strongest sector. Also, your technical-tone resumes outperform professional ones 2 to 1."
  • Pivot suggestions — "You keep getting turned down for Staff roles. Your profile is a much stronger fit at the Senior level right now. Want to adjust your targets?"
  • Salary negotiation — "They offered $130K. For your profile in this market, $145K is more in line. Here's a counter script with the numbers to back it up."
  • Interview prep — Mock interviews tailored to the role, common questions for the company, and a research brief
  • "Should I even bother?" — It'll be straight with you. "This one's a stretch. Here's what you'd need to close the gap. But also, here's a closer match you missed."
The more you use it, the better it gets. It learns which roles you skip, which companies get you excited, and what kind of feedback you're getting. Recommendations sharpen over time.

Key Architectural Decisions

Four calls that shape everything else.

How do we get job listings?

Aggregator APIs + User-Pasted URLs
Scrape LinkedIn/Indeed — Violates ToS. hiQ Labs paid $500K and had to destroy the data. Dead end.
Aggregator APIs — Adzuna, JSearch, Jooble, Reed, Arbeitnow, Remotive. Covers 70-80% of the market, completely legal.
User-pasted URLs — You find a job on LinkedIn, paste the link, the agent grabs that one listing to score and tailor for.

How do we submit applications?

Channel-Aware Autonomous Apply V3
User clicks Submit every time — Defeats the whole "wake up to 10 applications" promise.
Auto where safe, queued where risky — Email and career pages go out on their own. LinkedIn, Workday, Greenhouse get pre-filled and held for your approval.

How do we handle email?

User's Own Email via OAuth
Dedicated agent email — Employers see a weird address they don't recognize. Looks sketchy. Now you're checking two inboxes.
User's real email via OAuth — Gmail or Outlook with scoped permissions. Applications go out from your actual address. Optional +jobs alias if you want separation.

Which AI models do what? V3

OpenRouter Multi-Model Routing
Gemini Flash — Search, parsing, classification. Cheap and fast. Handles about 70% of all calls.
Gemini Pro / Claude Haiku — Job matching, scoring, skill gap analysis. Needs to think a bit but still cost-sensitive.
Claude Sonnet — Resumes, cover letters, career advice. The writing has to be good here.
Claude Opus — Tricky strategy questions and edge cases. Used sparingly.

Blended cost lands around $1.50-3/user/month because Gemini Flash carries most of the load.

Risk Register

What could go wrong, how bad it would be, and how we handle it.

CRITICAL

Platform Account Bans

If we automate LinkedIn or Indeed submissions directly, users get permanently banned. Over 461 tools have been blacklisted already.

Mitigation — We only auto-submit through safe channels. LinkedIn and Indeed apps get queued for the user's own tap. Zero ban risk by design.
CRITICAL

Legal Liability (ToS / Scraping)

hiQ v. LinkedIn ended with a $500K judgment and forced data destruction. Scraping is a legal landmine.

Mitigation — All job data comes from legitimate APIs or single-URL parsing that the user initiates themselves. We don't scrape anything.
HIGH

AI Hallucinating Credentials

36% of hiring managers say they reject AI-generated applications. If the AI invents a credential you don't have, the interview falls apart.

Mitigation — The writer agent can only use what's in your verified profile. Every generated doc shows a diff of what was changed. System prompts have hard guardrails against invention.
HIGH

Stale Salary Data

If the AI just guesses at salaries, users end up asking for too much or too little. Both are bad.

Mitigation — We pull real numbers from Levels.fyi and live job postings. Every estimate shows its source and when it was last pulled. Data refreshes monthly.
HIGH

Aggregator APIs Deprecate

Indeed already killed their API. Any of the others could do the same.

Mitigation — We pull from multiple sources and never depend on just one. User-pasted URLs work as a fallback no matter what.
MEDIUM

Email OAuth Verification Delay

Google's OAuth review process takes 4-6 weeks. If we don't start early, it blocks launch.

Mitigation — Start the verification process well before launch. Use minimal scopes. The tool works fine without email access, you just update statuses by hand.
MEDIUM

User Churn After Placement

Most job searches take 3-6 months. Once someone lands a job, they cancel.

Mitigation — "Career growth mode" keeps the tool useful after placement with salary benchmarking, skill tracking, and passive opportunity monitoring. Annual pricing and a referral program help too.
MEDIUM

GDPR / Privacy Regulations

Resumes are personal data. EU can fine up to 4% of revenue for mishandling it.

Mitigation — AES-256-GCM encryption at rest. Self-hosted option available. EU data residency for the cloud version. One-click data deletion and full DSAR compliance.

User Stories

Formal acceptance criteria. Click to expand.

U0 Signup & Onboarding

As a new user on any device, I want to sign up and build my profile in under 5 minutes so the agent can start working immediately.

  • Given I visit the web app, when I sign up, then I choose: upload resume, build from scratch, or explore first
  • Given I choose "build from scratch," when I answer guided questions, then the agent constructs a profile and starter resume
  • Given I complete my profile, when prompted, then I can connect email via OAuth for autonomous apply
  • Given I want email separation, when connecting, then I can opt into a +jobs alias
  • Given I add the PWA to home screen, then I receive morning briefings as push notifications
U1 Resume Upload & Smart Profile

As a job seeker, I want to upload my resume and have it parsed into editable fields so I don't have to re-type everything.

  • Given I upload a PDF/DOCX/image, when parsing completes, then all fields are pre-populated with confidence indicators
  • Given a badly formatted resume, when text extraction fails, then Claude Vision parses the visual layout
  • Given I save my profile, when I return on any device, then it's persisted and synced
U2 Salary & Level Calibration

As a job seeker, I want to know what I'm worth in different markets, backed by real data, so I don't undersell myself.

  • Given my profile is complete, when I request calibration, then I see salary ranges for 3+ regions with source attribution
  • Given my experience, then the agent recommends a seniority level with reasoning
  • Given arbitrage opportunities exist, then the agent suggests higher-paying regions with visa/relocation context
U3 Skill Gap Analysis

As a job seeker, I want to know which skills are worth learning and how much they'd actually increase my pay.

  • Given my target roles, when I request analysis, then skills are ranked by how often they appear in live listings
  • Given a skill gap, then I see the estimated salary bump, match rate improvement, and where to learn it
  • Given I have a rare skill, then it's called out as a competitive advantage
U4 Unified Job Feed

As a job seeker, I want all my jobs in one feed instead of checking five different sites.

  • Given I search, then results from all connected aggregators appear in one list with sources shown
  • Given I paste a job URL, then it gets pulled in, scored, and added to my feed
  • Given the same job is on multiple boards, then I see it once with all sources listed
  • Given any listing, then it has a 0-100 score with a plain-language explanation
  • Given my profile fits a role I haven't searched for, then the agent suggests I expand my search
U5 Autonomous Apply V3

As a job seeker, I want the agent to handle applications overnight and brief me in the morning on what happened.

  • Given 80%+ confidence on a safe channel, when the overnight cycle runs, then the agent submits on its own
  • Given 80%+ confidence on a risky channel, then it's queued for one-tap batch approval
  • Given 50-79% confidence, then it goes to a review bucket with draft materials
  • Given below 50%, then it's skipped but visible with reasons
  • Given the cycle finishes, then I get a morning briefing notification
  • Given I approve or reject apps over time, then the agent adjusts its scoring
  • Given I turn autonomous mode off, then everything waits for manual approval
U6 Email Integration & Response Tracking

As a job seeker, I want the agent to catch employer replies and update my pipeline so I don't miss anything important.

  • Given Gmail/Outlook is connected, when an employer replies, then the agent figures out what it is and updates the status
  • Given it spots an interview invite, then it offers to add it to my calendar and pulls together prep materials
  • Given nobody replies for 7 days, then it drafts a follow-up for me to review
  • Given I don't connect email, then I can still update statuses by hand
  • Given monitoring is on, then it only reads job-related threads, never personal email
U7 Career Advisor & Weekly Briefs

As a job seeker, I want data-backed career advice, not generic tips.

  • Given a week of activity, then the brief covers my response rates, which resume versions worked, strongest sectors, and what to do next
  • Given I keep getting rejected at a certain level, then the advisor explains why and suggests where to aim instead
  • Given I report an offer, then I get a negotiation script with real market numbers
  • Given I'm brand new with no history, then it gives general guidance based on my profile until it has enough data to get specific
U8 Billing & Subscription

As a user, I want clear pricing and no gotchas.

  • Given I sign up, then I get 14 days of Pro free, no credit card
  • Given my trial ends and I haven't upgraded, then I drop to Free (profile, tracker, and 5 tailors/month still work)
  • Given I upgrade, then I pay through Stripe (card, Apple Pay, Google Pay)
  • Given I cancel, then my data stays around for 90 days in case I come back
